FAQ

Deep cleaning questions

How is deep cleaning different from a regular clean?+

A regular clean maintains a property that is already in good order. A deep clean goes after what has accumulated: grease inside and on top of kitchen units, limescale on glass and taps, grout, silicone joints, and the areas behind and beneath furniture. It takes longer and usually needs more technicians.

How often should a Dubai home be deep cleaned?+

It depends on cooking habits, occupancy and how often the home is maintained in between. Homes with heavy cooking and several occupants need it more often than a lightly used one-bedroom. A practical approach is a deep clean when you notice grease or limescale returning, then regular visits to hold the standard.

Do I need to empty cupboards before the visit?+

For kitchen deep cleaning, emptying cupboards lets technicians clean the interiors properly. If that is not practical, tell us and we will focus on exteriors, tops and the areas around them. Clearing valuables and fragile items from worktops before the team arrives always helps.

Will you move furniture and appliances?+

Technicians move what can be shifted safely by hand so they can clean behind and underneath. Heavy or built-in appliances, and anything plumbed or fixed in place, are cleaned around rather than pulled out. If something specific needs moving, mention it when you book so the crew is sized for it.

Is post construction cleaning a one-visit job?+

Often, but not always. Fine cement and gypsum dust keeps settling out of the air after the first clean, so a second pass a few days later is sometimes sensible. We will tell you honestly at quoting stage if we think the property will need more than one visit.

Are the products safe for marble and natural stone?+

Yes. Marble, travertine and other natural stone are cleaned with pH-neutral products, not acidic descalers, which can etch the surface permanently. Tell us at booking which surfaces you have so the technicians arrive with the correct materials rather than substituting on site.
